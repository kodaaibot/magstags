Migrations

Technical Migrations

The migration is approved. The dev timeline is tight. And somehow the SEO requirements are still waiting for sign-off from three teams who each think it's someone else's responsibility.

I've led migrations for some of the UK's largest e-commerce and travel sites: platform moves, domain consolidations, CMS rebuilds. The SEO risk isn't the redirect map. It's everything that doesn't make it into the redirect map. I handle the architecture, the risk assessment, the stakeholder alignment, and the part where someone has to say "no, we can't launch on that date" and be right about it.

International

International & Multi-Market SEO

54 markets. 11 languages. A CMS that was built for one region and is now expected to handle all of them gracefully. It does not handle them gracefully. Multi-market SEO isn't just hreflang and translated keywords: it's governance, prioritisation, and figuring out which markets actually justify the investment.

I've managed organic search across dozens of markets simultaneously, including the messy reality of local teams, shared platforms, and content workflows that were never designed for scale. I help teams build the structure that makes international SEO manageable rather than something that slowly falls apart when nobody's watching.

Due Diligence

SEO Due Diligence & Pre-Acquisition Audits

The organic numbers look strong on the acquisition deck. Your job is to decide whether they'll still look strong 12 months after close. Whether the traffic is genuinely earned or propped up by a domain history that won't survive a rebrand. Whether the content driving that traffic is defensible or one algorithm update away from irrelevance.

I work with venture capital firms and investors to assess the real quality of a target's organic performance: not the vanity metrics, but the structural fundamentals. Traffic durability, technical debt, content defensibility, competitive positioning. The things that determine whether you're acquiring a growth engine or inheriting a problem.
